Email Marketing

Design & Development

I've worked in email marketing since 2012. My first entry-level position as a front-end website developer came to a quick end. The VP of my company at that time was doing all of the email campaigns for our clients and he was tired of it. You see, email (even to this very day) still uses the kind of HTML coding that we used back in the 1990s to make websites before CSS came to be.

So, using HTML Tables and inline CSS styles, I have worked through to the present day developing and honing these skills. My current job is designing and coding email templates for hoteliers worldwide. Sometimes I do them in Chinese, Japanese, Russian, French. I have clients in Canada, Singapore, Barcelona, Ireland, the United States and each one requires a lot.

Below are some examples of various projects I've developed for my hotel clients.

The Abbey:
Slice & Dice

Occasionally, a client will do their own creative and send us a PSD or flat JPG. They are images top to bottom including every bit of the text except for what you see in the CAN-SPAM footer at the very bottom and preheader at the very top. This client wanted a look in their email campaigns that standar email HTML coding and inline CSS just cannot accomplish on their own. Instead, we slice up the image and put it all back together in tables with a tags that take the reader to various landing pages on the client's website. We call these types of campaigns "Slice & Dice"
